## 1. Timeline and Voice Analysis of Lesson-5 Measure 1

### Observed Facts:
1. **Notes & Durations**: Under 4/4 meter with triplet scaling implemented (commit `3b138a7f`), the first measure contains **12 eighth notes** scaled to **320 ticks** each, plus a **1280-tick rest**.
2. **Voice Assignment**: All 13 elements (12 notes and the 1280-tick rest) are assigned to **Voice 1**.
3. **Overfull Measure**: The total duration sum for Voice 1 is:
   $$\text{Total Ticks} = (12 \times 320) + 1280 = 3840 + 1280 = 5120 \text{ ticks}$$

## 2. Origin & Intent of the 1280-tick Rest

- **Timeline Coalescence**: Due to close proximity in X coordinates (X delta $\approx 4.5$ points, which is less than the slicing threshold $X_{\text{tol}} \approx 5.1$ points), the timeline builder (`build_staff_timeline_preview`) grouped the sequential, arpeggiated eighth notes into chords.
- **Parallel vs. Sequential Discrepancy**:
  - The timeline preview treats these grouped notes as playing in parallel, resulting in a perceived notes-only measure duration of only **2560 ticks** ($8 \times 320$).
  - Recognizing a timing underflow ($3840 - 2560 = 1280$ ticks), the timeline builder automatically appended a `padding_rest` of **1280 ticks** to fill the measure.
  - When exporting to MusicXML, the builder does not mark these notes as chords. The MusicXML importer parses them sequentially, summing their durations to **3840 ticks** ($12 \times 320$), and then appends the **1280-tick** padding rest, creating the overfull measure of **5120 ticks**.
- **Conclusion**: The 1280-tick rest is a **false padding rest** generated by the timeline builder's slicing logic. There are no actual rests written in Measure 1 of the source PDF. The notes are intended to be played sequentially in Voice 1, completely filling the 4/4 measure (3840 ticks).
